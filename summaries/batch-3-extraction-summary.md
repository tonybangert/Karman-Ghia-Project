## The headline: GAP-03 is solved, and it rewrites the timeline

The complete photo of the machine shop invoice arrived in this batch. The mystery shop is **NV Automotive (Bill Shapley), 4580 E. Lake Mead Blvd #104, Las Vegas**, "Quality VW Racing Engines and Transaxles." Invoice #125, total $379.67, $200 deposit, paid cash. The fax number on the complete invoice matches the cropped fragment from Batch 1, confirming it's the same document.

And the date changes everything: **August 13, 2013**. The Type 4 head work happened roughly 18 months before the 2015 receipt cluster. This was not a one-year sprint; the engine build was underway in mid-2013.

The same shop appears again at the other end of the story: a second NV Automotive invoice (#100) for **two Weber carb rebuild kits, May 31, 2018**, which becomes the new latest-dated document. The documented build window now spans **August 2013 to May 2018**, with Bill Shapley's shop bookending the entire engine story.

## Other significant findings

**Spark plug spec recovered.** Butch's Speed Shop, May 2015: four Bosch W8DC plugs, the correct aircooled heat range. Now on the engine spec card. (Side note: this invoice is billed to "martin Mahan," likely a family member or counter-entry variant.)

**Instrumentation confirmed.** An Auto Meter 2258 electric temp sender (Butch's) plus a 300-degree oil temperature sender (Mid America Motorworks, Dec 2015). The car runs aftermarket gauges; identifying the gauge heads from dash photos is new GAP-20.

**First wheels evidence.** A 16-piece chrome 14mm lug bolt set from Mid America Motorworks (April 2015) implies aftermarket 4-lug wheels. Identifying them from photos is GAP-17.

**The JBugs interior saga fully reconciles.** Order 944952B (March 24, 2016) is the missing middle shipment: door panels and grey velour headliner. With A (Feb 23), B (Mar 24), and C (Mar 26) all in hand, every backorder, the -198.00 balance, and the -27.00 refund/recharge now close out cleanly. The two Batch 1 JBugs invoices also arrived as complete re-photos and were matched as duplicates.

**A second master cylinder appeared.** EMPI buggy master cylinder with reservoir, April 2016, fifteen months after the TRW unit from KGPR. Either a replacement, an upgrade, or the TRW didn't work out despite "customer says it will fit." Which one is on the car is GAP-19.

**Body-finishing micro-moment.** Two sequential Boulder Auto Parts invoices, two minutes apart (May 4, 2015): 3M fine-line refinish tape, then Permatex weatherstrip adhesive. Paint detail work and seal installation in the same afternoon.

**One new attribution flag.** The Drake Automotive insulating sheets invoice (Dec 2014, now the earliest parts purchase) is billed to "Bill Wilson, NV," not Mahan, though it lives in the Ghia folder and insulating sheets fit the interior work. GAP-18.

**Interior install dated.** Forty oval-head #6 trim screws from McFadden-Dale Hardware, May 10, 2016, right after the full interior kit arrived. The interior was going in that month.
