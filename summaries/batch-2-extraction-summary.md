## Batch 2 findings worth knowing

**1. The duplicate check worked.** One photo in this batch was a second shot of DOC-007 (invoice 2655-493365, the K&N kit). Matched by invoice number, not re-entered. As the folder grows, invoice numbers are the dedupe key.

**2. First paint-system evidence.** DOC-023 is a Boulder Auto Parts (CarQuest) paint department invoice for a Nason Select clearcoat activator (NAS 483-79). Nason is DuPont's refinish line, so the body almost certainly wears a DuPont Nason clearcoat. The store may still hold the color mix formula under customer number B4925, which would recover the exact paint recipe. That is GAP-13 and worth a phone call.

**3. The V-belt story resolved itself.** Gates 7370 purchased 2/18/15, returned and exchanged for Gates 7365 on 2/21/15 with the return authorization referencing the original sale. Final fitment from that exchange: Gates 7365. The Raby kit also includes a DTM-specific fan belt, so one open question is which belt serves which drive on the finished car.

**4. An attribution question surfaced.** Two items do not obviously belong to this car: a Type 1 (stock Solex) carburetor rebuild kit from Meyers Auto Parts (2/29/16), and a counter lookup against a "1967 Volkswagen Beetle" for the sealed beams. The headlamps fit the Ghia identically, so that one is just clerk convenience. The carb kit genuinely conflicts with the dual Weber setup. Either it predates/sits aside the Webers, or Mahan had another aircooled VW and some receipts in the folder may not belong to this car. Logged as GAP-16; worth keeping in mind as we process the rest of the folder.

**5. The timeline is filling in beautifully.** The receipt dates now sketch the build sequence: paint materials in late January 2015, an intense electrical/rewiring phase in February (including two O'Reilly trips in one day), fuel system plumbing mid-February, ignition and filters in March, gear lube (transaxle fill) mid-March, headlamps in April, copper spray sealant in late September 2015 (a plausible engine-assembly marker), and activity continuing through September 2016, which extends the documented restoration window.

**6. One receipt needs a re-shoot.** DOC-016 (O'Reilly 2655-492277, 3/28/15, about $48 net) has its line items cut off at the photo edge. GAP-14.
