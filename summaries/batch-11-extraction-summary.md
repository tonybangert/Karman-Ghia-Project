## The $500 car, on film

Three printed photographs survive with the project's papers (rephotographed; prints retained by the owner). Undated, but the condition places them at or near acquisition, before the 2014 Utah body/paint phase:

- **(a) Side profile, on a trailer**: heavily oxidized green paint, rust-through and primer patches dotted along the rockers and door bottoms, steel wheels with hubcaps.
- **(b) Rear three-quarter**: rear lower quarters in primer/bare metal, one taillight lens missing, rear bumper absent, exhaust pipe protruding.
- **(c) Front three-quarter**: nose and front valance damage, glass present.

## What the before-pictures prove

1. **The pre-restoration color was green** (oxidized; whether factory is unknown, GAP-01 still open for the paint code). The car's full arc is now photographed: green and rusted on a trailer, then silver-blue metallic and finished in the desert.

2. **The handwritten cost ledger reads like a captions list for these photos.** Every DOC-043 prep line maps to visible damage: media blast ($100), Bondo ($35) and Dolphin Glaze ($30), fiberglass ($10), 'Nose & fender & Hood Replacement parts' ($200, the photographed nose damage), primer stages ($125 + $100 final).

3. **The 'for iron' mystery resolves (GAP-23).** The car arrived on steel ("iron") wheels with hubcaps. The backordered 'CENTER CAP, CHROME, FOR IRON' parts and chrome decals on Mid America order 04682256-00001 were evidently dress-up for those original steel wheels, abandoned when the disc-conversion/alloy direction won out. With that, all three wheel-parts threads have explanations: 14mm bolts for the installed Fuchs-style alloys, iron caps for the original steels, Sprintstar caps for a set that never went on.

4. **GAP-16 gains a footing**: the project car is demonstrably this green Ghia coupe; the stray Type 1/Beetle receipts remain the open attribution question.
